Android help - AOSP / accessibility / audio

Audio too small

If you're experiencing audio that is too small, first try increasing your volume.
On your phone's side, there will likely be two buttons.
Press the longer one, towards the top of the phone, to increase volume.

If you cannot hear small details in your sound, Sound Amplifier may help.
Head to settings, by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Accessibility", then tap it.
Scroll down to find "Sound amplifier", and tap it.
You may be prompted to install the app. In this case, please go ahead and install it.

Connect your headphones or earbuds.
Afterwards, tap "Phone media" to make small sounds louder.
Alternatively, you may tap "Phone mic" to use your phone like a hearing aid.
When using the mic option, it is recommended to use wired earbuds or headphones to minimize delay.
Adjust settings below to your liking.

Unable to know what's playing

Android live captions can help you understand what's playing by transcribing your audio.
Head to settings, by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Accessibility", then tap it.
Scroll down to find "Live Caption", then tap it.

There are many options here.
Some may only be available in Google Pixel devices.
To stop using Live Captions, turn it off in settings.
Alternatively, when the captions appear, tap and hold to "grab" it, then swipe it all the way to the bottom of your screen.

I want my phone to help listen to things in real life.

You can use Live Transcribe to have your phone transcribe text
Head to settings, by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Accessibility", then tap it.
Scroll down to find "Live Transcribe", then tap it.
Allow microphone permissions while the app is being used.
You may also tap the cogwheel icon to access more settings.

From the cogwheel menu, you can also enable sound notifications.
This allows your phone to listen for doorbells, alarms and more on your behalf.
