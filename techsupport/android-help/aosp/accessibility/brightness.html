Android help - AOSP / accessibility / brightness

This guide will help you increase or decrease the brightness of your screen.
First, try adjusting you brightness from the quick settings screen.
This will allow a pretty wide range of adjustment.
Swipe down twice from the top of your screen to open quick settings.
Find the brightness slider, and move it to the left or right.

You can let your phone adjust to the brightness level of your environment.
To enable adaptive brightness, head into settings by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Display", then tap on it.
Find "adaptive brightness", and turn it on.
If you find your screen brightness keeps changing weirdly, you may turn the setting off.

If your screen is too bright even after pulling your slider all the way to the left, you can try "extra dim".
Head into settings by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Accessibility", then tap it.
Scroll down to find "Extra dim", and tap it.
You may configure the settings from here.
Toggle the feature on.
Use the "intensity" slider to adjust how dark your screen will be.
"Keep on after device restarts" will persist your settings after a reboot.
You may configure a shortcut if you desire.

Make sure to read through the description at the bottom to learn more about how to use the tool.
