Android help - AOSP / accessibility / magnify

Android has a built in tool to magnify content on screen.
Head to settings, by opening the settings app, or swiping down twice from the top of the screen and tapping the cogwheel.
Scroll down to find "Accessibility", then tap it.
Scroll down to find "Magnification", and tap it.

To enable magnification, tap on "Magnification shortcut".
On the pop up, select the option you'd like to use to enable magnification.
Tap "save".

To configure how magnification behaves, look at the menus below.
"Magnification type" allows you to choose whether the magnification should display a virtual "magnifying glass" you can drag around, or magnify the entire screen.
"Magnify typing" will make your screen follow the text input field while you type.
"Keep on while switching apps" will keep the magnification on while you switch to different apps.

Make sure to read through the description at the bottom to learn more about how to use the tool.
