Android help - AOSP / apps / install

Before we begin, I don't recommend installing the following apps:

  • QR code scanning apps - your camera should already have this function
  • Flashlight apps - your phone already has a feature to do that.
  • "Optimizer" apps - They usually don't work, and will slow your phone down.
  • Any apps that have the current year in the icon or name - They're likely clones of legitimate apps.
Use your own judgement.
If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
Fradulent apps can be dangerous.

Let's begin.
Open the play store on your device.
You may have to go into your app drawer to find it!
From your home screen, swipe up to open your app drawer.
It should show all of your installed apps.
Search for the play store, and tap it to open.
Alternatively, ask the Google Assistant, or scoll to find the app.

Tap the search bar on the top section.
(Depending on what google thinks today, it might also be on the bottom as a button.)
Search for the app you want to install.
Tap the app you want to install to open its details page.
If you'd like to go ahead with the install, tap the install button.

If you see a price instead of the install button, the app is not a free app.
Consider using an alternative, or if you're sure, follow the steps to add a payment method to your Google account.
